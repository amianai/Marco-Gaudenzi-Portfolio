### [**EasyMeds**](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/11l8trgUOSYG5D2OFqCFyUaAiCLW3FjSioFMegNup0yk/edit?usp=sharing)
*A pharmacy companion app focused on reducing counter time and managing cognitive load.*

* **The Problem:** Waiting in line at the pharmacy and lacking immediate information on drug availability, generic alternatives, prices, and allergens. Managing multiple prescriptions and physical health cards is also a fragmented, frustrating process.
* **My Role:** End-to-end UX/UI design. I handled the domain analysis, defined personas, mapped user flows, and designed the final high-fidelity screens, maintaining strict adherence to Material Design 3 guidelines. 
* **Key Features:**
  * **Smart Profiles & Caregiver Mode:** Users input their allergies once, and the app automatically filters all searches. A quick toggle disables this global filter when buying meds for someone else. It also centralizes multiple health cards and tracks prescription status.
  * **Constraint-Driven Design:** We had a strict constraint: do not disrupt the physical pharmacy's business model. Instead of building a generic "skip-the-line" digital ticket, we optimized the transaction itself. By allowing upfront booking and payment, we severely cut down time spent at the counter without bypassing the pharmacist's role.

### [**Pearl companion robot**](https://canva.link/sb6p1aon6siv8d6) - [**Figma File**](https://www.figma.com/file/RjhurQp3duxQ3cvzITSL07?node-id=0:1&locale=en&type=design)
*A smart home companion designed for safety, remote monitoring, and accessible daily assistance.*

* **The Problem:** People living alone often lack a sense of security and a reliable way to monitor their home or manage daily alerts. Traditional smart home hubs provide utility but lack a reassuring, physical presence that feels natural to interact with.
* **My Role:** UX Research & UI Design. I led user interviews to gather real-world needs, mapped out the core user flows, created the visual models, and validated the interactions through targeted usability testing.
* **Key Features:**
  * **Non-Verbal & Expressive UI:** Designed a simple, friendly interface relying on expressive eyes, soft shapes, and light signals. This ensures clear, immediate communication that feels natural even without relying on voice commands or complex screens.
  * **Retractable Projector:** Integrated the concept of a retractable projector, allowing the robot to transform any surface into a temporary information hub whenever needed, without cluttering the physical hardware with large displays.

### [safeHer](https://drive.google.com/file/d/19PjybbMzcCAFdruYqY08L4xsuvLuhcWJ/view?usp=sharing) - [Research Report](https://docs.google.com/document/d/16qxu85xTobD1bsMLR5KQV23fHAejhuZkYZ42rPDa_Xs/edit?usp=sharing)

*A systemic service design project tackling gender-based violence and urban safety through research and ecosystem mapping.*

* **The Problem:** Female university students, especially commuters, often feel unsafe navigating cities at night. The lack of reliable urban lighting, the fear of verbal or physical harassment, and the hesitation to call traditional authorities due to slow response times or fear of dismissal create severe psychological friction and limit personal freedom.
* **My Role:** User Researcher & Service Designer. I contributed to the empirical research phase (distributing and analyzing surveys to the target demographic), mapped the resulting data into Value Proposition Canvas frameworks (Customer Jobs, Pains, Gains), and applied the CPS (Client-Problem-Solution) methodology to frame the core issues.
* **Key Features:**
  * **Ecosystem Approach:** Instead of stopping at a simplistic "live tracking app," we proposed an integrated service ecosystem. This includes community-driven urban mapping (highlighting safe paths based on lighting and open businesses) and a network of affiliated safe spaces (like late-night bars) acting as immediate, decentralized emergency responders.
  * **Pain-Driven Ideation:** The design directly responds to the emotional pains mapped during research, such as the anxiety of walking alone and the friction of contacting law enforcement, by shifting the focus to immediate, local community support.
